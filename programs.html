Physics Program

Preparing students for success in STEM with hands-on physics lessons

At Sigma Learning, we provide physics lessons that help students develop a solid foundation in STEM. Our program is designed to prepare incoming 8th graders for the challenges of high school science, and equip incoming 9th graders with the core concepts they'll need. For students ready to take on more advanced material, we also offer AP Physics 1 prep, ideal for those heading into 11th and 12th grade.

In our program, students will explore key physics concepts through real-world applications, experiments, and problem-solving. Whether it's understanding motion and forces, the principles of energy, or the foundations of electricity and magnetism, our goal is to make physics engaging and accessible for everyone.

Examples of Topics Covered:

  • Forces and motion (e.g., Newton's laws)
  • Energy and power (e.g., conservation of energy)
  • Electricity and magnetism (e.g., circuits, electromagnetism)
  • Waves and sound (e.g., properties of waves, resonance)
  • Introduction to AP Physics 1 concepts (e.g., kinematics, dynamics, rotational motion)

Grade Levels: Incoming 8th–9th graders, AP Physics 1 for incoming 11th–12th graders

Class Format: Weekly lessons, hands-on experiments, interactive problem-solving, and group activities

Mathematics Program

Strengthening problem-solving skills and preparing students for their next math challenges

Our math program is designed to build a solid foundation for students from incoming 6th to 9th grade. We help students prepare for key math levels such as Math 6/7, Math 7/8, AGS 1, AGS 2, and AGS 3. Our goal is to develop critical thinking and problem-solving abilities through engaging lessons and interactive exercises that make learning math enjoyable and accessible.

Examples of Topics Covered:

  • Math 6/7: Fractions, decimals, ratios, basic geometry, and introductory algebra
  • Math 7/8: Proportions, integers, linear equations, geometric shapes, and probability
  • AGS 1: Solving equations, graphing, linear functions, and basic algebra
  • AGS 2 & 3: Advanced algebraic concepts, quadratic equations, factoring, and more
  • Pre-algebra & High School Prep: Introduction to polynomials, inequalities, and functions

Grade Levels: Incoming 6th–9th graders (Math 6/7, Math 7/8, AGS 1, AGS 2, AGS 3)

Class Format: Weekly lessons, interactive exercises, group activities, and math challenges

Chess Program

Enhancing strategic thinking, focus, and problem-solving through chess

Our chess program teaches students not only how to play but how to think critically and strategically. Through lessons and live games, students will learn the basics of chess, as well as advanced tactics and strategies that will help them think ahead and make better decisions both on and off the board.

Whether you're a beginner or already have some experience, our program is designed to challenge students and help them improve their chess skills. We focus on developing patience, concentration, and a growth mindset, all while having fun and competing in a supportive environment.

Examples of Topics Covered:

  • Chess Basics: Pieces, movements, and basic rules
  • Tactics: Pins, forks, skewers, and discovered attacks
  • Strategic Planning: Opening principles, controlling the center, piece coordination
  • Endgames: Checkmating patterns, king and pawn endgames
  • Advanced Strategies: Positional play, pawn structures, and sacrifices

Grade Levels: Incoming 6th–9th graders (for all skill levels)

Class Format: Weekly lessons, hands-on practice, chess puzzles, and live matches

Art & Crafts Program

Encouraging creativity, self-expression, and hands-on fun

Our Arts & Crafts program is all about giving students a space to explore their creativity and imagination. Through engaging weekly projects, students will learn to work with different materials, techniques, and tools to create their own unique works of art.

From painting and sketching to DIY crafts and design projects, this program is perfect for anyone who enjoys being creative and working with their hands. No prior experience needed — just bring your curiosity and willingness to try new things!

Examples of Activities:

  • Drawing and painting
  • Paper crafts and origami
  • DIY projects and creative reuse
  • Holiday- and theme-based crafts

Grade Levels: Incoming 6th–9th graders

Class Format: Weekly hands-on sessions with guided instruction, open-ended projects, and time for creativity and fun
